Marriage Registration & Post-Wedding Administration

Organise official questions, documents and post-wedding records while relying on the responsible government authority for the actual process.

General information, not legal advice

Requirements depend on the applicable law, state or union territory, district, jurisdiction and the couple's circumstances. Confirm the current process directly with the authorised Marriage Officer, registrar or official government portal before acting.

Start with the correct authority

Central laws provide a legal framework, while registration delivery and detailed requirements may depend on state rules and local jurisdiction. Search the National Government Services Portal, then confirm the registrar or Marriage Officer responsible for your address or place of marriage.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using another state’s document list or fee as if it were national
  • Confusing solemnisation, registration and certificate-copy services
  • Assuming a universal witness count
  • Entering identity information on a lookalike or commercial agent site
  • Failing to check spelling and dates before signing
  • Booking international travel while identity records are inconsistent

Helpful answers

Frequently asked questions

Is this legal advice?

No. It is an organisational guide. Use the applicable government portal and obtain qualified legal advice for individual questions.

Are documents and witness rules the same across India?

No. The responsible authority and requirements can vary by law, state, district and circumstances.

Should I pay an agent?

Use the official portal or office first. Verify any fee and approved payment method with the authority, keep receipts and never share identity documents through an unverified service.

What is the difference between a ceremony and registration?

A ceremony follows the couple’s religious, cultural or civil plan; registration is an administrative process under the applicable law and authority. The relationship between them depends on the legal route.

Where should I begin?

Use the National Government Services Portal or the official state, district, municipal or revenue website, then confirm jurisdiction with the registrar or Marriage Officer.
