Indian Wedding Planning Guide

A practical timeline for bringing families, ceremonies, guests and vendors together without losing sight of what matters to you.

Indian weddings can include several events, many decision-makers and guests travelling from different places. Good planning does not mean making the celebration impersonal; it creates the space to be present.

Begin with five major decisions

✦

1. Shared priorities

Each partner names three must-haves and three areas where they can be flexible.

✦

2. Comfortable budget

Agree on a total range, contributors, approval process and contingency.

✦

3. Guest estimate

Create a realistic range before looking at venues or catering.

✦

4. Place and season

Consider travel, weather, availability, accessibility and family needs.

✦

5. Celebration format

Choose the events and approximate number of days before booking.

A 12-month planning timeline

12 months before: set the foundation

  • Hold a calm priorities and budget conversation.
  • Draft the guest list by family group and identify uncertain invitations.
  • Shortlist the city, dates or date range; consult your officiant for religious requirements.
  • Visit venues and compare what each quotation includes.
  • Create one shared planning folder and a decision log.

9 months before: secure the essentials

  • Confirm venue terms, deposits, access hours and cancellation conditions.
  • Book priority vendors such as planner, caterer, photographer and key entertainment.
  • Begin attire research, especially if custom work is planned.
  • Identify travel and accommodation needs for close family and older guests.
  • Outline each event and its likely guest count.

6 months before: shape the guest experience

  • Finalise ceremony and reception formats with both families.
  • Choose the invitation direction and collect accurate names and addresses.
  • Develop décor, menu and music briefs tied to your budget.
  • Plan transport routes and accessibility support.
  • Review legal marriage requirements separately with the relevant authority.

3 months before: turn plans into schedules

  • Send invitations according to guest travel needs.
  • Complete menu tastings and attire fittings.
  • Confirm ceremony items with the priest, pandit or officiant.
  • Build event-by-event run sheets with vendor arrival times.
  • Assign family contacts so the couple is not handling logistics on the day.

Final 30 days: confirm, do not reinvent

  • Collect RSVPs and dietary or accessibility notes.
  • Give final counts only after checking contract deadlines.
  • Confirm outstanding balances and use traceable payment records.
  • Prepare seating, transport, rooming and emergency contact lists.
  • Pack documents, attire, jewellery and ceremony items by event.

Wedding-week checklist

  • Share the final run sheet and contact list with family leads and vendors.
  • Check weather and adjust only practical details.
  • Confirm who holds rings, documents, payments and emergency supplies.
  • Allow time for meals, hydration, rest and private moments.
  • Nominate one person to handle last-minute questions.

Helpful answers

Frequently asked questions

When should we start planning an Indian wedding?

Many families begin 9 to 12 months ahead, especially for a multi-event celebration or popular season. A shorter timeline can work when priorities, guest numbers and decision-makers are clear.

Who should decide the wedding budget?

The people contributing financially should agree on a comfortable limit, what it includes and who approves changes. Keep this conversation private, specific and free from comparison.

How can families divide responsibilities?

Assign one accountable lead for each area—such as guest travel, ceremonies, venue coordination or payments—and keep a shared contact list and deadline tracker.

Do we need every traditional event?

No. Include ceremonies and celebrations that are meaningful, practical and agreed by your families. Confirm religious requirements with your priest, pandit or officiant.
