Suggested booking order

1. Define the weddingAgree on events, guest range, location, priorities and budget ceiling.
2. Secure constrained servicesConfirm venue, officiant and date-dependent essentials before secondary briefs.
3. Book experience and productionCompare catering, photography, planning, décor, entertainment and beauty scopes.
4. Confirm logisticsArrange transport, accommodation, clothing, jewellery and detailed deliveries.
5. Conduct the final handoverShare one run sheet, contact list, payment record and escalation plan.

Questions every vendor should answer

  • What exact service and deliverables are included?
  • Who will attend, and can the team change?
  • Which costs, taxes and overtime are excluded?
  • What equipment, access, meals, power or workspace do you need?
  • What happens after postponement, cancellation, illness or equipment failure?
  • What are the payment dates and refund conditions?

Budget tips and common mistakes

  • Compare like-for-like written proposals.
  • Keep contingency outside contracted commitments.
  • Record every approved change and revised price.
  • Do not choose solely from social-media highlights.
  • Do not assume a venue or planner has checked every subcontractor.
  • Avoid paying untraceable amounts without receipts.

Helpful answers

Frequently asked questions

Does ShaadiGuide list or recommend businesses?

No. This is an educational directory of vendor categories and booking questions, not a marketplace or ranking service.

Which vendor should be booked first?

Secure services constrained by date and capacity first—often venue and key ceremony providers—then use the confirmed schedule to brief other vendors.

Should we pay a deposit without a contract?

Request written scope, payment and cancellation terms before paying. Keep receipts and seek qualified advice for unclear or high-risk terms.

How do we compare quotations?

Give every vendor the same brief and compare inclusions, exclusions, staffing, quantities, taxes, overtime and cancellation terms—not only headline price.
