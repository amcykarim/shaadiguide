How to Choose a Bridal Outfit

Prepare a clear brief, compare like-for-like proposals and record the service, people, price and protections before booking.

What this vendor does

Designers, boutiques or tailors provide garments, alterations, finishing and agreed accessories for specific events.

When to book6–12 months before for custom work

Typical planning timeline

ResearchWrite one event brief and review complete examples of comparable work.
ShortlistShare identical dates, locations, hours, quantities and priorities.
ContractResolve scope, team, price, payments, changes and cancellation in writing.
Final handoverConfirm access, contacts, schedule, balances and contingency plan.

Questions to ask before booking

  • Is the piece ready-made, made-to-measure or custom?
  • What measurements, fittings and delivery dates apply?
  • Which fabric, lining and embellishments are specified?
  • What alteration and defect process applies?
  • Can the outfit support sitting, walking and ceremony movement?

Budget considerations

Include garment, customisation, blouse or lining, embroidery, alterations, shipping, pressing, accessories and rush charges.

Contract checklist

  • Legal or trading name and current contact details
  • Exact events, locations, dates and service hours
  • Named lead, staffing and subcontracting terms
  • Itemised deliverables, quantities and exclusions
  • Fees, taxes, deposits, payment dates and receipts
  • Overtime, travel, meals, power and access requirements
  • Postponement, cancellation, substitution and refund terms
  • Delivery, damage, liability and usage rights where relevant

Warning signs

  • No written delivery or fitting schedule
  • Reference images substituted for specifications
  • Final fitting too close to travel

Practical tips

  • Ask for complete work or service examples comparable to your scale.
  • Give cultural and ceremony notes directly; do not expect the vendor to define family traditions.
  • Keep one written decision log and confirm price-changing requests.
  • Assign one event-day contact who is not the couple.

Helpful answers

Frequently asked questions

When should we book a bridal outfit?

A useful planning target is 6–12 months before for custom work, but availability depends on location, season, scale and complexity. Compare written scopes before paying.

How many quotations should we compare?

Compare enough written proposals to understand scope and market differences. Use the same event details and inclusions so the comparison is meaningful.

What should be written into the contract?

Record dates, locations, hours, named services, quantities, staffing, fees, taxes, payment dates, overtime, cancellation, postponement, substitutions and delivery obligations.

Is this a vendor recommendation?

No. ShaadiGuide does not list, rank or endorse businesses. This page is educational preparation for your own checks and decisions.
