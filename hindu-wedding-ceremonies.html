Meaning before the moment

Hindu Wedding Ceremonies & Rituals

A respectful overview of ceremonies you may encounter before, during and after a Hindu wedding.

Traditions are not universal. Names, order, participants and interpretations vary by region, community and family. Confirm religious details with your priest, pandit, officiant or elders.

A flexible ceremony journey

Some celebrations include only a few of these moments; others take place across several days. Use this overview to recognise terms, prepare questions and decide what is meaningful for your families.

01

Roka

A family acknowledgement of the proposed union in some North Indian communities; format and significance vary.

02

Sagai

An engagement gathering that may include rings, gifts, blessings or family introductions.

03

Ganesh Puja

A prayer seeking blessings and the removal of obstacles, often held before wedding rituals.

04

Mehndi

Application of henna, commonly paired with music, refreshments and time with family and friends.

Read the Mehndi planning guide →
05

Haldi

A turmeric-based blessing ceremony observed in varied forms, often separately by each family.

Read the Haldi planning guide →
06

Sangeet

A music and dance celebration; today it may range from an intimate family evening to a staged programme.

Read the Sangeet planning guide →
07

Baraat

The groom’s wedding procession in communities where it is observed, arriving with music and dancing.

Read the Baraat planning guide →
08

Milni

A welcoming meeting of corresponding relatives from the two families in some traditions.

09

Jaimala or Varmala

An exchange of flower garlands, often symbolising mutual acceptance.

Read the Jaimala and Varmala guide →
10

Kanyadaan

A ritual interpreted differently across families; some retain it, adapt its wording or choose alternatives.

11

Mangal Phera

Circling the sacred fire while making prayers or vows; number and order can vary.

12

Saptapadi

Seven steps taken together with commitments associated with married life in many traditions.

Understand the seven steps →
13

Sindoor

Application of vermilion as a marital symbol in communities where it is practised.

14

Mangalsutra

A sacred necklace tied or presented in several communities, with regional variations in form and meaning.

15

Vidaai

An emotional departure or transition after the ceremony, expressed differently by modern families.

Read the Vidaai family guide →
16

Griha Pravesh

A welcome into the marital home, often involving blessings and symbolic entry rituals.

17

Reception

A social celebration for greeting guests, dining and congratulating the couple.

Questions to ask your officiant

  • Which rituals are essential within our family tradition?
  • What do the Sanskrit or regional-language verses mean?
  • Who participates, and where should each person sit or stand?
  • Which items must we arrange, and which will the officiant provide?
  • How long will each part take, including transitions?
  • How can the ceremony be explained clearly to guests?

Helpful answers

Frequently asked questions

Is this the order of every Hindu wedding?

No. The list is an overview, not a universal sequence. Events, names, timing and meanings vary greatly by region, sampradaya, community and family.

Who can explain the religious meaning for our ceremony?

Speak with the priest, pandit or officiant conducting your wedding. Ask them to explain the sequence and translations before the event so both partners can participate knowingly.

Can a family adapt a ceremony?

Many families discuss adaptations for time, accessibility, gender equality or personal values. Make those decisions respectfully with your partner, elders and officiant.
