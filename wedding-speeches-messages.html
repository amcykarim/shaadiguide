A simple speech-writing framework

  1. WelcomeName the occasion and acknowledge the room.
  2. ConnectionExplain your relationship without giving a full biography.
  3. One truthShare one quality or safe memory that reveals character.
  4. GratitudeThank people specifically and proportionately.
  5. CloseOffer a concise wish, toast or original blessing-style line.

Length and delivery guidance

One spoken minute is usually about 120–150 words. Practise with a timer, print in large type, mark pauses and speak more slowly than feels natural. Look up between paragraphs rather than trying to memorise every sentence.

What to avoid

  • Stories the couple has not agreed can be shared
  • Jokes about money, ex-partners, fertility, appearance or marriage as a burden
  • Assumptions about gender roles or family structure
  • Film dialogue, song lyrics, famous quotations or copied poetry
  • Invented religious translations or lines described as scripture

Helpful answers

Frequently asked questions

How long should a wedding speech be?

Many speeches work well at two to four minutes. Confirm the programme limit, practise aloud and leave space for pauses.

Can I tell a funny story?

Yes, if the humour is kind, relevant and comfortable for both partners. Ask before sharing anything private.

Are the blessings on this site religious text?

No. ShaadiGuide blessing-style examples are original non-scriptural wording. Confirm formal religious language with the officiant or elders.

Do the builders use AI or save personal details?

No. They combine your entries with predefined templates in the browser and do not submit or save the fields.
