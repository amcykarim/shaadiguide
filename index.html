Helpful answers

Frequently asked questions

Where should we begin planning an Indian wedding?

Start with five shared decisions: priorities, a comfortable budget range, guest estimate, location and the events that matter to your families.

Does every Hindu wedding follow the same ceremonies?

No. Ceremony names, sequences and meanings vary by region, community and family. Confirm religious details with your priest, pandit, officiant or elders.

How early should we book wedding vendors?

Popular venues and priority vendors are often considered 9 to 12 months ahead, but availability varies. Compare written quotations and contracts before paying deposits.

Can the budget calculator include several events?

Yes. Enter combined costs, or estimate events separately when guest counts differ and then add the relevant totals together.

May we adapt the invitation wording examples?

Yes. Personalise every example with your names, hosts, event details and family voice, then proofread carefully before sending.
