Bank and Insurance After Marriage Checklist

An educational review of contact, address, nominee, beneficiary and KYC questions after marriage.

General informationPrimary sources onlyRequirements vary
General information, not legal advice

Requirements depend on the applicable law, state or union territory, district, jurisdiction and the couple's circumstances. Confirm the current process directly with the authorised Marriage Officer, registrar or official government portal before acting.

Requirements vary

Do not treat examples on this page as a universal document list, fee schedule, deadline or appointment rule.

Review, do not assume changes

Marriage does not mean every account must become joint or every nominee must change. Inventory accounts and policies, then decide what review is relevant.

Bank questions

Check contact details, address, name consistency, KYC status, nominees, standing instructions, loans and credit accounts directly with each regulated institution. Do not share OTPs, PINs or passwords.

Insurance questions

IRDAI explains that policy details can include nomination and address and that servicing requests may go through the insurer or, where used, an insurance repository. Confirm the process with the specific insurer.

Keep evidence

Retain official acknowledgements and note which records were accepted. Verify updated statements rather than assuming a request succeeded.

Scam prevention

Use contact details printed on the institution’s official website or documents. Do not permit remote access to a device or transfer money to “unlock” an update.

Protect identity records

Do not upload identification numbers or document scans to ShaadiGuide. Use a verified government portal and check the web address before entering personal information or paying.

Helpful answers

Frequently asked questions

Is this legal advice?

No. It is an organisational guide. Use the applicable government portal and obtain qualified legal advice for individual questions.

Are documents and witness rules the same across India?

No. The responsible authority and requirements can vary by law, state, district and circumstances.

Should I pay an agent?

Use the official portal or office first. Verify any fee and approved payment method with the authority, keep receipts and never share identity documents through an unverified service.

When was this page verified?

The linked primary government sources were checked on 20 July 2026. Recheck them because procedures can change.
